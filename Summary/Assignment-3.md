# Assignment 3: Individual Contribution Report in the Mid-term Project (LLM Course)

## 1. Objective

Assignment 3 requires each student to **individually submit a report on their personal contribution** to the group’s Midterm Project.

The purpose of this assignment is to verify each member’s **actual participation, specific responsibilities, and concrete work**, supported by verifiable screenshot evidence.

This assignment does not primarily re-evaluate the group’s final model performance. Instead, it focuses on:

1. whether the student genuinely participated in the Midterm Project;
2. whether the student completed specific and verifiable tasks;
3. whether the submitted evidence sufficiently supports the claimed contribution;
4. whether the division of labor within the group was clear and fair, without free-riding, false claims, or complete non-participation.


**DDL: 2026.05.11 (AOE)**

---

## 2. Basic Requirements

1. **Each group member must submit an individual contribution report.**
2. The report must focus on the student’s actual work in the Midterm Project.
3. The report must clearly state:

   * what tasks the student was responsible for;
   * what specific work the student completed;
   * how that work contributed to the project;
   * how the student’s work differed from that of other group members.
4. All claimed contributions must be supported by **screenshots as evidence**.
5. Vague statements such as “I participated in training,” “I helped with tuning,” or “I joined discussions” are not sufficient unless they are explained in concrete detail and supported by evidence.

---

## 3. Report Content Requirements

Each student’s report is recommended to include the following parts:

### 3.1 Basic Information

1. Name
2. Student ID
3. Group number or group name
4. Main role in the project

### 3.2 Overview of Personal Contribution

Provide a short summary of your main responsibilities in the Midterm Project, such as:

* data cleaning and filtering,
* training sample construction,
* SFT / RL experiment design and execution,
* inference and validation,
* log organization and result analysis,

### 3.3 Screenshot Evidence

Each major contribution must be accompanied by corresponding screenshots. Screenshots should be clear, complete, and identifiable, and should reflect at least some of the following:

* your account, name, or student ID;
* commit history, code records, scripts, experiment logs, job dashboards, or file modification history;
* data processing procedures, intermediate results, training configurations, experiment outputs, or leaderboard submissions;
* other visible traces showing your actual work and participation.

### 3.4 Personal Reflection

Briefly summarize your contribution, the problems you encountered, how you addressed them, and your objective view of the team collaboration.

---

## 4. Requirements for Screenshots and Evidence

1. **Screenshots are the core supporting evidence for this assignment. Any claimed contribution without valid screenshot evidence will not be counted as an effective contribution.**
2. Each screenshot should preferably include a short caption explaining what it proves.
3. Screenshots should include identifiable information whenever possible, such as time, file name, task name, log content, or account information.
4. Acceptable forms of evidence include, but are not limited to:

   * code commit records;
   * screenshots of notebooks or scripts being run;
   * before/after screenshots of data processing;
   * training logs, loss curves, or evaluation results;
   * server job records;
   * model submission records;
   * document editing records;
   * any other materials that clearly demonstrate the student’s actual work.
5. Screenshots must not be misleadingly cropped, stitched, altered, or fabricated.
6. If a screenshot cannot reasonably show who completed the work, its evidential value may be reduced.

---

## 5. Requirement for Distinguishing Contributions Within the Group

1. Each group member’s reported contribution should have **clear boundaries and a distinct scope**.
2. **If the contributions and screenshots submitted by different group members do not overlap, no penalty will normally be applied.**
3. If multiple members submit the same or highly overlapping screenshots, descriptions, or results without clearly distinguishing their individual roles, such contributions may be treated as **duplicate claims** and may receive deductions.
4. If multiple members contributed to the same shared group outcome, each member must explain their own specific part and provide independent evidence showing their individual contribution.
5. Statements such as “we did it together,” “everyone participated,” or “we all discussed it” are not sufficient unless the individual roles can still be clearly identified.
